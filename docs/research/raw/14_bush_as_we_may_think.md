## Section 6 — verbatim extracts (the Memex)

### On how the human mind works
> "The human mind does not work that way. It operates by association. With one item in its grasp, it snaps instantly to the next that is suggested by the association of thoughts."

### On associative indexing
> "Selection by association, rather than by indexing, may yet be mechanized."

### Memex — the definition
> "A memex is a device in which an individual stores all his books, records, and communications, and which is mechanized so that it may be consulted with exceeding speed and flexibility. It is an enlarged intimate supplement to his memory."

### Context (not verbatim — paraphrase of the surrounding text)
Bush contrasts traditional indexing systems — which require following alphabetical or numerical paths — with associative selection that mirrors how human memory actually functions through interconnected trails rather than rigid hierarchical classification.
